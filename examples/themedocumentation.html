Documentation

Omero

For the style of the first theme we took inspiration from the Greek inscriptions and, more specifically, from those of the classical period (V-IV a.C.). Why greek inscription? Because with the first epigraphs there was the diffusion of an alphabetic writing system in which at each phoneme corresponds a grapheme. This phenomenon was revolutionary because the previous forms of writing, where the signs still represented an object or an idea or a combination of syllables were abandoned.

The features

The epigraphs were usually inscribed on durable materials such as stone or marble: text-shadow and opacity CSS properties allowed us to reproduce this effect on the screen.

Ancient Geek is the font we used for the whole document since it seemed to us the most suitable to reproduce the epigraphic aspect of the characters in the classical period, characterized by a great composure and formal perfection. In addition, we decided to set the same font size for all the elements in order to keep the graphic homogeneity that characterizes the epigraphs.

Another important feature is the layout of the text, developed in Athens during the classical period, known as “stoichedico”. Each line of the text basically has the same number of characters and each letter of each line is placed vertically with respect to the corresponding letter of the next line, so as to create something like a grid. Css letter-spacing property was extremely useful to try to ricreate this feature.

Divina Proportione (Leonardo)

This theme is inspired by the notes of XVI Century scientists. In particular, Leonardo and his disciples- Cesare Cesariano, Francesco DI Giorgio Martini, Luca Pacioli. Their studies fully mirrored the concept of "scientia", as something that involves all disciplines.

"The Ancients elaborated all their works according to these proportions: the perfection of the circle, the principle of all regular bodies, and the equilateral square."

Luca Pacioli

The naturalistic view

The theme features a parchment paper as the background, with a chosen font that simulates Leonardo's Handwriting. Images are side by side to text, as in Leonardo and his disciples' true notes. The vertical line beside the images is necessary to mirror how the scientists structured their notes- neat text that separates graphic content by this fine line. Because drawings at that time were as important as their description, hovering on them brings them back to life, revealing their true colors. Following the same logic, one of Leonardo's drawings is reproduced in the background, as a gif.

Proportions in the XVI century were taken very seriously: underneath them, it was possible to look at the geometric figures God had chosen to create Earth and its living creatures. This belief is represented in the page layout of Leonardo and his disciples' notes: a column reserved to drawings, a central part to describe their observations, a footer to summarize and add content that did not belong to the main text.

XVI scientists'notes surprisingly taught us a lot about design. One of the articles on the matter can be found here: redundancy is key when studying concepts, use your imagination and any sketch needs annotation.

The scarlet letter

This theme is in regards to the 19th century which is remembered as one of the longest rules of Britain from Queen Victoria, who was with important and really influential role in the artistic and cultural spheres. Her reign was known as the Victorian era and is popular because of writesrs as Charles Dickens.


The Victorian era conveys a distinct typographic heritage linked indelibly to heavy “Egyptian” Slab serifs and Clarendons. The calligraphic scripts were also widely used in this specific period. The headlines that inspired me were the ones of some English newspapers in that era which were written all in uppercase, using a font that is richly decorated with some bigger than the usual letter spacing in between and having a drop cap in different style at the beginning of each paragraph.

Calligraphic scripts that work are difficult to come by, but Aquiline Two makes a pretty good stab at recreating antique-style handwriting, exactly because of this reason I chose it for this project.

Apart from the above sources of inspiration in regards to the choice of colors, I have chosen the typical royal violet and crimson. All of the images are transformed in black and white in order to correspond to the certain period and bring some nostalgia about it. The borders of the images are rounded because from the layout examples that I have used for inspiration, I found out that they preferred sharped and remembering type of font and uppercase headline, but in regards to the images was preferred the more subtle approach – smaller images, rounded borders. Sometime even the font was put around rounded images as in the example of the cover of the book of Charles Dickens, The Christmas Carol.

My text is positioned in one column and reminds of the style of writing a letter. The letters in this period were still really popular as a form of correspondence. Each of my paragraphs starts with a drop cap, which was a top trend in that period. For the drop cap was used a font which is rich in decorations and makes the first letter stand out from the rest of the paragraph. In my case I decided to go even further and put it in different color from the rest of the text, so that here we have also a connection with the novel the Scarlet letter, also written in the Victorian era.

Bauhaus

This theme is principally inspired by the original Bauhaus journal (1926-1931). Directed by many important figures of the movement, it came to include new features each time a new number was published.

"In order to use color effectively it is necessary to recognize that color deceives continually."

Josef Albers

A geometric view

The theme mostly mirrors the journal, with its big, bold headers in the Sans-Serif typeface, a very low line-height, and a very small font size -everything lowercase- for the paragraphs.

However, there are some additions that reflect the Dessau period too, as Bauhaus has recently turned 100. You can find them out resizing the screen and pushing the three buttons for three colour combinations. Bauhaus artists worked a lot on complementary colours, and we tried to recreate those studied by Josef Albers and Piet Mondrian.

Swiss Style

This theme is inspired by the Swiss designers during the 1950s. Swiss Design was a movement that took hold in the 1950s in two Swiss art schools, the kunstgewerbeschule in Zurich, led by Josef Müller-Brockmann, and the Allgemeine Gewerbeschule in Basel, led by Armin Hofmann.

"The Swiss style (International Typographic Style) was a major force in graphic design in the 50s through the 80s. Swiss Design uses asymmetric layouts with text aligned flush-left, ragged-right; sans serif typefaces like Akzidenz Grotesk and, later, Helvetica (originally called Neue Haas Grotesk).

The photography

Other important features are the use of photographs instead of illustration; and, most importantly, the deployment of a mathematically determined grid to determine the placement of design elements—a method that remains extremely important to this day in web design.The ‘50s are characterised by the use of light and very intense colours.

The Neon 90s

The Neon 90s theme was inspired by the improvement in the technologies in the 90s of the 20th century. The video games became really popular in the 90s like Tetris, Pacman, Super Mario, Sonic the hedgehog were born and remained one of the most iconic and favourite to millions of people worldwide.


Apart from this in the 90s the world became much more open-minded and the young people wanted to be more provocative and express their mindset better by wearing neon colors, which were making them stand out in the crowd. Because of this fashion trend I have used quite a lot these colors that remind me of the start of something new and setting end to the traditional colors, but trying something more expressive and provocative.

The layout of my paragraph was inspired by the game Tetris where you have different blocks in bright neon colors and you have to set them at the right place. Scrolling through this topic the user could get an experience of being in a game when reading a normal article exactly because of the unusual positioning of the paragraphs, seeming as if they are floating on the screen and the vivid colors of the fonts as well as the borders of the images that also remind us a well-known game Pac-Man.

The fonts used in this stylesheet could be considered as belonging to the large group of the so-called Computer fonts. They are quite more subtle, with straight lines, almost lacking any decoration. However, they are distinctive and well representing the technological progress in this era and the focus on different further technological improvements.

Tomorrow

What are the trends for the next few years? No one can say. What we tried to do was analyze the present and translate it into something better starting from our personal experience. We tried to achieve a balance between minimalism and brutalism in order to ensure the readability and usability on one hand, and to create visual tension on the other to grab person’s attention, as Pangram Pangram Foundry suggests.

As it is clear, fonts play a major role in such a design. The bolder the better, as they say—and with typography, there’s no limit to how loud you can be. We used VelodromaBasic, a wide, loud, san serif font and we put it in bold in order to let the convayed message to scream and shout. But the widest is not the only thing you get with Velodroma. You get regular and wide too in one font, so you can combine them together. Indeed, we also see sans serifs as a new standard for “clean” work. As Papaelias says, “Modern San Serifs have become a voice for the non-corporate mainstream voice like Helvetica”.

By adding a dashed border to each paragraph, we wanted to make the composition look ordered, airy and soft and we tried to put into practice the so- called theory of loci, according to which we can we can memorize things more easily when we associate them with a specific place. The :hover selector and CSS animations gave us the ability to create a dynamic, interactive design which wants to make feel the reader involved. In addition, we tried to create a 3D effect on the text. using text-shadow CSS property and groove style for borders.

The day after tomorrow

The prediction we have made from 2040 and beyond is that we will get some result from the search on aliens by looking them as intelligent civilizations. As the internet transformed society over the past few decades, it will give us the capacity to process more data about ourselves, the planet we live on, and the universe than has ever before been possible. So, it is assumed that alien-produced electromagnetic signals will be discovered by 2040.

”Brain chip implants which will be capable of reading the brain's signals, or stimulating the brain with its own signals will be used by almost everyone. With these chips, the people will be able to get the alien-produced signals and communicate them as it is shown with a ‘voice recognition image’ inside the documents.

VR-optimized websites will allow even more possibilities for interactive storytelling. An animation of an alien is added for the representation of interactive storytelling.
